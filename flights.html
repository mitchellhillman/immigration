Booking Flights & The Move

When and how to book the one-way move once you have an interview date. Embassy-agnostic community advice. Not legal advice.

The core strategy

  • Don't book until you have an interview date.
  • Book a flexible / refundable ticket — the peace of mind is worth it, and it avoids a last-minute fare scramble.
  • Book the initial date far enough out to cover processing (e.g. ~2 weeks after the interview), then move it earlier if the visa comes back sooner.
  • Wait until the visa is in hand before actually changing the flight.

How flexible tickets actually work

  • You can move the flight earlier or later — even months out.
  • Most airlines waive the change fee on a flexible fare, but you may owe the fare difference if the new flight costs more. Often it's a wash; sometimes a small top-up.
  • Refundable/flexible fares can cost more up front — weigh that against the risk of buying a normal ticket for a visa that isn't guaranteed yet.

Some people skip booking entirely until the visa is in hand, specifically to avoid the denial risk. Valid too — it's a cost-vs-certainty trade-off.

Estimating interview → visa in hand

  • Look at recent timelines from your own embassy — interview-to-passport varies a lot by post.
  • Some posts are running social media checks that can add up to ~a week.
  • Others are just slower to print/return passports. Build that buffer into the initial booking.

Summer 2026 timing

  • World Cup + peak tourist season make summer flights packed and pricey (esp. into hubs like LA).
  • Booking early — flexibly — beats hunting for last-minute seats when everything's full.
