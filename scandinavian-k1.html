K-1 Fiancé(e) Visa — Scandinavia

Consular processing through the US Embassy Stockholm. Compiled from community interview reports (Sweden, Finland, Denmark). Not legal advice.

Case status sequence

NVC case # assigned STKxxxxxxxx → Status "Ready" → Sent to embassy → Interview → Approved → Issued (CEAC) → UPS package → Passport in hand

Timing risk: Some applicants waited up to a month after the case number was assigned before being sent to the embassy (missing transfer windows). "Issued" on CEAC ≈ visa printed into the passport. Packages have arrived via UPS within ~2–3 days of issuance.

The officer will say the passport can take up to ~2 weeks to come back, but in practice many receive it within the same week. CEAC may briefly read "refused" before flipping to "issued" — that's a normal administrative state, not a denial. Once you hold the sealed visa packet you can board your flight; reports note no extra ID/passport checks at the gate.

Medical exam book it late, not early

  • Do not book the medical too early. Wait until the case status reads "Ready" / sent to the embassy — booking 2 months out risks the case lagging at DOS.
  • Gothenburg allows booking with the case number, but waiting for "Ready" is the safer play.
  • Cost reported: ~800 SEK total, plus ~80 each for 2 vaccines.

Lost medical (AOS step): the sealed medical that travels with your visa packet has been getting lost fairly often lately. There's no copy to request — if it's lost, you redo the exam with a civil surgeon in the US. This can trigger an RFE at the adjustment-of-status stage, so don't be alarmed if it happens.

Interview day — Embassy Stockholm logistics

  1. Embassy opens at 08:00; you can queue outside before then.
  2. Separate, shorter line for US citizens + K-1 applicants — not the "glass house" line.
  3. When called: show passport, state purpose, show bag/jacket contents. Bring minimal (a small tote: documents, passport, wallet, phone, keys).
  4. Belongings go through an airport-style scanner. Follow the white dots on the ground to the building.
  5. State purpose again inside → queue for the cashier.
  6. Cashier verifies your documents → directs you to window 7.
  7. Leave your passport at window 7 → wait for your name.
  8. Document handoff: confirm receipt of medical results; confirm you understand your rights on entry; confirm you can travel home without your passport.
  9. Swear to be truthful → interview.

What to expect: the interview runs ~10 minutes or less. Reports describe it as more of a formality — they're looking for red flags, not trying to catch you out. It happens in a shared room, so others in the queue can overhear the questions. At the end you may be asked to make your social media public.

Documents to bring

  • DS-160 confirmation page (with barcodes)
  • 2 passport photos
  • I-134 (Affidavit of Support)
  • Sponsor's financial statements (e.g. tax return)
  • Photos of you and your fiancé(e) together (may be requested)
  • Letters of intent to marry — not asked for in at least one recent case, but bring them anyway.

Reported interview questions

  • Have you lived in any country other than [home country]?
  • How did you meet?
  • When did you first meet in person?
  • How many times have you visited each other?
  • What does the petitioner (beneficiary's partner) do for work?
  • If approved, would you be afraid to travel back to [home country]?
  • Have you been in Africa in the past year? Planning to travel there before entering the US?
  • Ethnicity / origin (asked, but not treated as a big deal where parents hold local passports).

The "Africa" question isn't random. It's triggered by what's in the system: recent travel history (e.g. a trip to Egypt), or ethnicity / parents from a travel-ban country. They generally want your honest answer to something they already see — not a curveball. Most questions focus on the petitioner (name, job, how/when you met, number of visits).

Tips: keep answers short, prepare thoroughly, dress neutral — clothing doesn't matter, just don't distract from the purpose.

Travel notes

  • Summer 2026 fares are high. One-way sometimes beats round-trip for the move.
  • Reported: ~$650 one-way w/ 2 checked bags (Expedia); +$200 for a 3rd bag (~$850 total). Others ~$1,500 one-way.
  • Living near Stockholm? You may be able to pick the visa up directly rather than waiting on UPS.
