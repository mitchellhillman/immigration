AOS Guide — Adjustment of Status (I-485)

The post-marriage green-card filing for K-1 entrants. Community-compiled notes on documents, name changes, medical, biometrics, and RFEs. Not legal advice.

Medical validity

  • For AOS purposes the medical is valid ~1 year — don't panic if the date on it looks sooner.
  • The 6-month figure people quote is the window to enter the US on the visa, not the AOS validity. Different clock.

SSN & name change

  • You can request a new SSN card in your married name directly on the I-485 — there's a checkbox for it. Check it and the new SSN card comes with your green card.
  • Alternatively, change the SSN name before the green card by bringing your marriage certificate to an SSA office.
  • When you first apply for an SSN, the form's first question is the name to show on the card — you can adopt a spouse's surname there.

State nuance: in Michigan, your SSN-card name must match your ID to get a driver's license. One applicant kept their maiden name on the SSN and requested the change on the I-485, to keep the names aligned and get the license right after the AOS NOA1. Check your state's rules before deciding the order.

Filling out the I-485

  • Use Adobe (Acrobat) Reader for the PDF.
  • If some fields won't accept typing, it's fine to fill what you can digitally and hand-write the rest — or just print and fill the whole thing by hand. Both are accepted.

I-864 / affidavit — tax documents

  • Instructions allow an IRS transcript OR a photocopy of your own records, but people have been getting RFEs for using photocopies — the safer choice is an IRS tax transcript.
  • If the sponsor is well above 125% of the poverty guideline, submitting the most recent year (transcript or copy) plus the W-2 and 1040 is generally enough. When in doubt, include the transcript.

Biometrics

  • Beneficiary only attends.
  • The appointment notice asks for details (height, weight, eye color, etc.) — fill it in beforehand.
  • At the ASC there's a check-in kiosk; it directs you where to go.
  • Wear your best smile — that photo goes on your green card.
  • Rescheduled to a different ASC? Whether the interview later routes from the new center or reverts to your zip-code default depends on the ASC — it should direct you.

RFEs & receipts

  • An RFE can happen at any stage — before or after biometrics, or after the interview. It varies; getting one isn't a verdict.
  • The receipt number typically arrives by text a few days after USCIS receives the package.

Packet assembly

  • Paperclips are fine for organizing the packet — no need for staples or fasteners.
  • Labeling documents is voluntary. Some put the applicant's name + A-number in each document's header; others don't label at all. Either is fine — do whatever keeps it organized for you.
